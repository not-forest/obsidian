## 1.1 What is computer vision

#### Humans
> How we (humans) see it - effortlessly, fully. We can tell the shape or translucency of each petal in a vase of flowers. See every segment of the picture, recognize people on images, guess their names and emotions.

#### Machines
> Researchers in computer vision have been developing, in parallel, mathematical techniques for recovering the three-dimensional shape and appearance of objects in imagery.

Despite all advances, computers aren't able to do more than a two-year old human being.

#### Why so difficult?

Because it is an *inverse* problem, in which we seek to recover some unknowns given insufficient information to fully specify the solution. Therefore *physic-based* and *probabilistic models* are used or *machine learning* from large sets of examples, to choose between potential *known* solutions.

> Forward Models - models, which are usually developed in physics (radiometry, optics, and sensor design) and in computer graphics. It models how objects move and animate, how light reflects off their surfaces, how it is scattered by atmosphere, to create an illusion of what we see in a real world.

#### Where is computer vision used?
- Optical character recognition (OCR): reading handwritten codes on letters;
- Automatic number plate recognition (ANPR);
- Machine inspection: repid parts inspection for quality assurance using vision to measure tolerances on aircraft wings or automation machine;
- Object recognition for automated checkout lanes and fully automated stores, hotels, services;
- Warehouse logistics: autonomous package delivery and parts picking by robotic manipulators;
- Medical imaging: registering pre-operative and intra-operative imagery or performing long-term studies of people's brain morphology as they age;
- Self-driving vehicles;
- Photogrammetry (3D model building from photography);
- Motion capture: captures actors for computer animations;
- Surveillance: monitoring public places, analyzing traffic, safeness;
- Biometrics and fingerprint recognition;
- Stitching: turning overlapping photos into a single seamlessly stitched panorama;
- Exposure bracketing: merging multiple exposures taken under challenging lightning conditions (strong sunlight and shadows)into a single perfect picture;
- Morphing: doing literally any magic with any picture;
- 3D Modeling: converting one or several photos into a 3D model format for further use or 3D printing;
- Video match move and stabilization;
- Photo-based walkthroughs: navigatinga large collection of photos like if you are walking there in real-time;
- Face detection/recognition;
