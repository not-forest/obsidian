## Kultur og språk

> [!question]- Det er vanlig med kjernefamilier i Norge. Det skjer ikke ofte at mange generasjoner, for eksempel besteforeldre og deres barnebarn, bor under samme tak. En interessant ting er at det er fire ord som kan beskrive besteforeldre: mormor -> mora til mora; morfar -> faren til mora; farmor -> mora til faren; farfar -> faren til faren; De vet du presis hvilken person det er snakk om! I tillegg bruker nordmenn ofte "bonuspappa" i stedet for stefar, og "bonusmamma" i stedet for stemor.
> Nuclear families are common in Norway. It is not often that many generations, for example grandparents and their grandchildren, live under the same roof. An interesting thing is that there are four words that can describe grandparents: grandmother -> mother to mother; paternal grandfather -> mother's father; grandmother -> mother of the father; paternal grandfather -> the father of the father; You know exactly which person it is! In addition, Norwegians often use "bonuspappa" instead of stepfather, and "bonusmamma" instead of stepmother.
